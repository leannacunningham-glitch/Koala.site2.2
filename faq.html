Common Questions About Koalas

  • Are koalas bears?

    No, koalas are not bears. They are marsupials, which means they carry their young in a pouch. They are more closely related to kangaroos and wombats.

  • Why do koalas sleep so much?

    Koalas sleep up to 22 hours a day to conserve energy. Their eucalyptus leaf diet provides very little nutrition, so they rest to maintain their energy levels.

  • What threats do koalas face?

    Koalas face threats including habitat loss, disease, climate change, and bushfires. They are currently listed as vulnerable to extinction in many parts of Australia.

  • How long do koalas live?

    In the wild, koalas typically live 10-12 years. In captivity, they can live longer, up to 18 years or more.

  • What can I do to help koalas?

    You can help by supporting conservation organizations, reducing your carbon footprint, and raising awareness about koala protection efforts.
