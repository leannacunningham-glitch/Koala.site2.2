Koala Characteristics

Koalas are medium-sized marsupials that typically weigh between 4 to 15 kilograms. They have a stocky body, large round ears, and a black nose.

Key features of koalas include:

  • Strong limbs for climbing trees
  • Sharp claws for gripping branches
  • Excellent sense of smell to select eucalyptus leaves
  • Dense fur for protection from temperature changes

Habitat and Diet

Koalas live in the eucalyptus forests of eastern Australia. They spend most of their time in trees, where they find their primary food source: eucalyptus leaves.

Koalas are very selective eaters and will only consume leaves from certain eucalyptus species. They have a specialized digestive system that helps them break down these toxic leaves safely.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Koalas are primarily nocturnal animals, meaning they are most active during the night. During the day, they rest and sleep for up to 22 hours!

These solitary creatures communicate with each other through vocalizations. Male koalas make loud bellowing calls, especially during breeding season.

Conservation Efforts

Koalas face many threats in the modern world. Donating to the Koala Foundation helps support critical conservation and research initiatives.
